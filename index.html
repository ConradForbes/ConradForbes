03 The Lab

Growing meat
from scratch.

Before working in data, I spent time in biology. My first substantial research project was in cell-based agriculture, looking at how to use brewing by-products to create cellulose scaffolds for cultured meat.

The work sat between materials science, biology, and process engineering. We were designing structures at the microscale to support living tissue, where small details made a noticeable difference.

It made me more comfortable working between disciplines, and more aware of how much perspective you gain by looking at a problem from different angles.

04 The Shift

From molecules
to systems.

Moving from biotech into data engineering did not feel like starting again, more like applying the same way of thinking in a different context. The habits that mattered in the lab, such as reproducibility, traceability and clarity about what you are measuring, turned out to matter just as much in reporting, analysis and data infrastructure.

Data became the medium, but the underlying mindset stayed familiar: understand the system, work within constraints and make decisions that hold up in practice.

Produced comparative models that highlighted differences in flow patterns and vessel loading between normal and post-TBI states, supporting further investigation into injury mechanisms.

CFD FEA Data modelling

About

Working across
data and decisions.

I work across data engineering, reporting, visualisation and automation, with a background that started in biomedical engineering and biotech research before moving into data systems. That mix has shaped how I approach problems: technically, but always with the end use in mind.

At KPMG, I have worked on data platforms, reporting workflows and stakeholder-facing tools used across complex organisations. I am most interested in the point where good data, clear communication and practical delivery come together.

A lot of my work sits between disciplines: understanding requirements, shaping information, improving processes and building systems that are reliable enough for others to depend on.

Outside work, sport has been a steady thread for me, from rugby to rowing and now triathlon. I like the mix of discipline, teamwork and gradual progress that each has demanded at different points in my life.

How I build

  • Clarity before complexity. Whether it is a pipeline, a dashboard or a process, the work should be understandable before it becomes sophisticated.
  • Understand the system, not just the task. Good decisions usually depend on seeing the upstream context, downstream impact and the trade-offs in between.
  • Design for the next person. Useful work is work that someone else can trust, maintain and build on.
  • Make improvement practical. The best solutions do not just look good in theory; they reduce effort, improve visibility or make better decisions easier in practice.
